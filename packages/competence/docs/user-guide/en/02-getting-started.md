# Getting Started

This chapter gets you into the application and oriented: how to sign in, what the Dashboard shows you, how the sidebar is organized, and how to adjust the interface to your liking.

## Signing in

You sign in with your organization account through your company's single sign-on — there's no separate registration or password to create for this application specifically. Depending on how your organization has set things up, this is typically a Microsoft Entra (Azure) sign-in, though the exact method depends on your deployment.

The moment you sign in, the application works out which capacities you hold — Employee, Manager, and Supervisor — from your position in the organization chart, while Team Member is conferred when you're picked for a specific evaluation's feedback team. There's nothing for you to configure yourself.

> **Note:** If sign-in fails, that's an account issue the application itself can't resolve — contact your administrator.

## The Dashboard

The Dashboard is the first screen you see after signing in, built to answer "what do I need to do right now?" at a glance.

- A **greeting card** shows a time-of-day greeting with your name and the status of your own most recent evaluation, plus — when you have one in progress — an **Open My Evaluation** button that takes you straight to it.
- A **cycle card** shows the current appraisal cycle's ID, a progress bar for how much of the cycle's time has elapsed, and its three key dates: when it started, the manager-review deadline, and when it closes.
- A row of **stat cards** adapts to what you do:
  - As an **Employee**, you'll see how much peer feedback has been submitted (of what was requested), how many of your own competencies are graded so far out of the total, how many days remain until the manager-review deadline, and how many of your teammates have started their own evaluations.
  - As a **Manager** or **Supervisor**, you'll instead see your team's evaluations broken down by count — in total, and by how many are open, in review, or ready.
- A **Tasks for you** panel is your personal to-do list — pending peer reviews, rounds gone overdue, interview notices, and similar items. Click any task to jump straight to the screen where you act on it.
- A **Recent Activity** panel lists recent evaluation events, so you can see what's been happening lately without opening individual evaluations.

## Finding your way around

The sidebar on the left is how you get everywhere in the application. What you see there depends on your capacities — a plain Employee sees a shorter list than a Manager or Supervisor does.

| Sidebar section | Item | What it is | Who sees it |
|---|---|---|---|
| Workspace | Dashboard | Your landing page | Everyone |
| Workspace | Org Chart | Your organization's reporting structure | Everyone |
| Workspace | My Evaluation | Your own current evaluation | Everyone |
| Workspace | My Scores | Your own results, once available | Everyone |
| Workspace | Availability | Your interview-availability calendar | Managers |
| Workspace | Interviews | The interview scheduling and closure hub | Managers and Supervisors |
| Manage | People | The employee management screen | Managers and Supervisors |
| Manage | Cycles | Appraisal cycle management | Supervisors |
| Manage | Oversight | The stall-recovery screen for stuck evaluations | Supervisors |
| Insights | Cycle analytics | Whole-cycle statistics | Supervisors |
| Insights | Team analytics | Statistics for your reporting line | Managers and Supervisors |
| Insights | Trends | Cross-cycle statistics | Supervisors |
| Administration | Configuration | Application configuration | Administrators only |
| Quick Links | Process Guide | A guided walkthrough of the appraisal process | Everyone |
| Quick Links | Help | This guide | Everyone |

Whichever screen you're on, its name appears at the top of the page in the topbar; your own name and stage-level sit at the bottom of the sidebar.

## Personalizing

A button near the bottom of the sidebar switches between a light theme (**Daylight**) and a dark theme (**Glass**) — its icon and label always show you which theme you'd switch to next. Use whichever is easier on your eyes.

> **Tip:** If you regularly move between a bright office and a dim one, try both — many people find Glass easier on the eyes later in the day.

Prefer more room for the page content? Click the arrow at the top of the sidebar to collapse it down to icons only; click it again to expand it back.
