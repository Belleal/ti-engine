# FAQ & Glossary

This chapter closes out the guide with two things: quick answers to the questions people ask most often, and a glossary defining the terms used throughout — a quick reference for whenever you want to double-check exactly what a word means.

## Frequently asked questions

### Why can't I see my manager's grades yet?

Your manager's grades stay hidden from you until your evaluation reaches Ready. From that point on, they're visible alongside the team's cumulative grade — never earlier.

### Can I ever see how a specific teammate graded me?

No — individual peer grades are never shown to anyone but the person who gave them, not even your manager. What you see instead, once your evaluation reaches Ready, is the team's single combined cumulative grade.

### Can grades be changed after submitting?

No — grades are locked in the moment a round is submitted; nobody edits them afterward. If a round stalls instead of being completed, a Supervisor has recovery paths for it — waiving a stuck self-round or completing a stuck manager round — rather than a way to edit grades directly.

### What happens if I miss my self-evaluation deadline?

A draft save or a submission after your self-evaluation deadline is rejected outright. If you genuinely can't finish in time, a Supervisor can waive the round once the deadline has passed so your evaluation isn't held up — your self-grades are then excluded from scoring entirely, never treated as a zero.

### Does a skipped team round hurt the score?

No. When a team round wasn't requested — or was finalized without any submissions — it's excluded from the score calculation entirely, and the remaining sources are renormalized to cover the full result, rather than the missing round counting as a zero.

### What does "Not Utilized" (N) do to the score?

It carries a weight of zero, so that competency contributes nothing to the score, positive or negative. Use it whenever a competency genuinely doesn't apply to the work being graded, or wasn't demonstrated during the period — not as a way to avoid grading something.

### Who sees my written self-evaluation comment?

Your manager sees it, as part of their review. Team members never do, and that never changes.

### Who conducts my interview?

Whoever owns the calendar slot that gets booked for your interview — most often your own manager, but the Supervisor can book any manager's open slot, so it's occasionally someone else. Either way, you're notified once the slot is booked, well before the interview itself.

### Why won't the cycle lock?

Locking runs a full validation pass, and any failure blocks it: a baseline not covering all nine subcategories, a resolved set over the cap, a competency drawn from outside its family's pool, or a family that's included but not yet configured. Whatever it finds is grouped by family so it's clear what to fix.

### Why is a chart cell empty in analytics?

Analytics screens never display a group smaller than three people. Rather than risk identifying someone by working backward from a tiny statistic, the application suppresses that cell entirely and leaves it empty.

### When do I see the interview feedback and my goals?

Only once your evaluation is Closed. From that point on, the interview's written feedback, your next-period goals, and any Performance Improvement Plan all appear together on My Scores.

### Can a closed evaluation be reopened or edited?

No. Closing is the final, irreversible step in the process — once a Supervisor closes an evaluation, nothing about it can be reopened or changed afterward.

### What is a Performance Improvement Plan?

It's an optional, formal plan that can be attached to an evaluation's interview outcome — written feedback and next-period goals cover the basics, and a Performance Improvement Plan adds a documented, structured layer on top when the situation calls for it. Whoever records the outcome decides whether to include one.

### How do I get administrator access?

You don't request it inside the application — Administrator status is a separate list of trusted accounts kept by your operations team. Ask them directly if you need your account added.

## Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| Appraisal cycle | The recurring period during which evaluations run, moving one-way through Planning, Active, and Closed; only one cycle is Active at a time. |
| Active Competency Set | The specific competencies that apply to a role family and specialization for a given cycle — the baseline plus whatever the specialization adds. |
| Baseline | The competency selection that applies to everyone in a role family regardless of specialization; it must cover all nine subcategories. |
| Specialization | An optional narrower focus within a role family; an employee without one set is treated as a generalist. |
| Generalist | An employee within a role family who has no specialization set. |
| Competency pool | The fixed set of competencies a role family may draw on; the picker and the cycle-lock validation only allow choices from within it. |
| Stage-level | The combination of level and stage (for example J2 or S3) that reflects seniority and sets each competency's relevancy weight. |
| Relevancy | How much a competency counts toward the score at a given stage-level, defined by its archetype curve. |
| Grade | The S / R / U / N rating given to a competency by the employee, the team, or the manager. |
| Threshold | One of the five score bands, from Weak to Outstanding, that a final score is read against. |
| Snapshot | The competency set — and its relevancy weights — frozen onto an evaluation the moment it starts, immune to any later configuration change. |
| Collective team evaluation | The default team-feedback mode, where team members grade by subcategory instead of competency by competency. |
| Cumulative team grade | The single combined grade calculated per competency from every team member's submission; individual team grades are never shown. |
| Conducting manager | The manager who owns the calendar slot booked for a given interview, whether or not they're the employee's own manager. |
| Structural Supervisor | A Supervisor by virtue of organization-chart position — the top manager, or a direct report whose own sub-organization runs at least two management levels deep; the role can't be taken away. |
| Granted Supervisor | A Supervisor role given to someone by a structural Supervisor; the same powers, except managing other people's roles, taking effect at the grantee's next sign-in. |
| Oversight | The screen where a Supervisor finds every stalled evaluation in the active cycle and recovers it — waiving a self-round, completing a manager round, or withdrawing the evaluation. |
| Waive (self round) | A Supervisor action that excuses a stalled, deadline-passed self-assessment from scoring so the evaluation can move on. |
| Withdraw | A Supervisor action that cancels an evaluation outright, at any active status; irreversible, and it immediately frees the employee for a new evaluation. |
| Closure | The Step 8 record of the interview's outcome — written feedback, next-period goals, and any Performance Improvement Plan — visible to the employee only once the evaluation is Closed. |
| PIP | Short for Performance Improvement Plan: an optional, formal plan that can be attached to an evaluation's interview outcome. |
